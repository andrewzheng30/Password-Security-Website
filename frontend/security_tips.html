Password Security Tips

  • Passwords should be at least 8 characters long.
  • Include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters.
  • Include numbers and special characters, like !, @, #, $, etc.
  • Avoid using easily guessable information, like your name, birthdate, or common words.
  • Consider using a passphrase instead of a single word for added security.
  • Use different passwords for different accounts.
  • Consider using a password manager to generate and store strong passwords.

  • Two-thirds of Americans use the same password across multiple accounts.

  • 59% of US adults use birthdays or names in their passwords.
  • 13% of Americans use the same password for every account.

Why Password Security is Important

Ensuring strong password security is crucial in protecting your personal and sensitive information from unauthorized access. Weak passwords are susceptible to hacking attempts, leading to identity theft, financial loss, and other cybercrimes. By using strong, unique passwords and regularly updating them, you can significantly reduce the risk of security breaches and safeguard your online accounts and data.
